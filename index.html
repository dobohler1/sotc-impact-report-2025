Our STEM Journey

This year, Science on the Court (SOTC) deepened its impact through a dynamic partnership with the Boys and Girls Club of San Leandro, serving students at Bancroft and John Muir Middle Schools. Our program blended basketball training, STEM learning, and mentorship to engage middle schoolers both on and off the court.


Students didn't just shoot hoops—they built and programmed robots, explored real-world physics, and applied math through the lens of sports. A major highlight was our field trip to Tesla, where students witnessed innovation firsthand and imagined their place in the future of technology.

🎯 Educational Impact Analysis

With Cohen's d effect sizes averaging 0.65 for Math and 0.58 for Computer Science, students gained approximately 5.2 additional months of math learning and 4.6 additional months of CS learning compared to typical classroom instruction. These medium-to-large effect sizes demonstrate educationally significant gains, with John Muir cohorts showing particularly strong academic acceleration.

📝 Attendance Context

Tier 1 students (≤25% attendance) typically attended only 1-2 sessions, not giving the program a fair opportunity to demonstrate its value. This limited exposure prevents meaningful skill development and relationship building with instructors.

Note on Bancroft Spring 25: We experienced temporary enrollment decline due to competition from a popular wrestling class. However, students returned once wrestling concluded, demonstrating continued interest in our program.

Key Program Findings

🎯

Significant Academic Acceleration

Students gained 4-5 additional months of learning in both Math and Computer Science (Cohen's d = 0.58-0.65), demonstrating educationally meaningful impact.

🔄

STEM Identity Transformation

31% of students shifted toward STEM career consideration, while 100% reported increased interest—showing both immediate engagement and lasting identity change through hands-on learning.

🏀

Sports-STEM Integration Success

The basketball-STEM hybrid model maintained 84% "very enjoyable" ratings while achieving strong academic gains, proving sports can enhance rather than compete with STEM learning.

🌟

High-Engagement Dosage Effect

65% of students achieved high engagement (≥50% attendance), with John Muir Fall 24 reaching 84% in the highest tier, showing the program's ability to sustain student interest over time.
